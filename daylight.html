Context
Going solar is one of the most opaque, high-stakes purchases a homeowner ever makes — tangled financing, high-pressure sales, and almost no visibility once the panels are on the roof.

Daylight is building a consumer energy platform to make that whole experience clear, fair, and human. I lead product across it.
Problem
The work lives on two hard surfaces. On the business side, installers and sales teams move a homeowner through a long, fragile pipeline — lead, quote, contract, credit, design, permitting, install, activation — each step with its own states, handoffs, and ways to stall.

On the consumer side, homeowners are left in the dark: confusing bills, no real view into what their system produces, and silence after the sale.
Role
I own 0-to-1 product strategy across design, engineering, and execution — translating a messy, regulated, multi-party business into product surfaces people can actually trust. I partner across leadership, engineering, design, and go-to-market to turn strategy into shipped product.
Two surfaces, one system
B2B — Origination

Portals that carry a customer from lead through quote, contract, credit, design, documentation, install, and payments. Product judgment lives in the workflow states, permissions, operational handoffs, and review loops.

B2C — Homeowner app

Clear visibility into the energy system, billing, usage, production, referrals, onboarding, and what happens after install. Bringing transparency to a category that often feels opaque.

Business side
I designed the origination and installer workflows that carry a customer from lead to activation — modeling the states, permissions, document collection, and operational handoffs that keep a deal moving without dropping it. Getting the workflow logic right is what lets sales orgs and installers move fast without things falling through the cracks.
Consumer side
I shaped the homeowner experience: clearer billing, real visibility into energy production and usage, onboarding, referrals, and the whole story after install. The goal is simple — take a category that feels predatory and opaque, and make it feel transparent and trustworthy.
